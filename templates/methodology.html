Match prediction model

01
Historical data

Premier League match results from 2016/17 to present are loaded. Goals scored and goals conceded are extracted per match and assigned home or away, forming each team's base ratings.

02
Base attack and defence ratings

ATT and DEF ratings are computed per Team. ATT is the average goals scored both home & away; DEF is the average conceded. These ratings represent the team's underlying quality.

ATT = (avg_home_goals_for + avg_away_goals_for) / 2
DEF = (avg_home_goals_against + avg_away_goals_against) / 2
03
Recent form adjustment

Form ratings are computed from the most recent 20 matches and blended with the base ratings. The blending parameter α (default 0.65) controls how much weight is placed on recent form versus long-run quality. Higher α makes the model more reactive to recent form.

blended_att = (1 − α) × base_att + α × recent_att
blended_def = (1 − α) × base_def + α × recent_def
04
Computing Expected goals (xG)

Team xG is derived by blending two methods:

  • Multiplicative xG — ATT rating × opponent DEF rating. Captures the interaction between attack quality and defensive vulnerability.
  • Poisson-matched xG — a binary search finds the xG value that, when simulated through the scoring model, reproduces the team's expected goals given their ATT rating (538 method).

The two are blended (default β = 0.8 weighting toward multiplicative) and adjusted for team-specific home field advantage (derived from the last 20 home vs away matches, capped at ±0.3 goals) and any manual adjustments for material squad changes.

xG = (1 − β) × poisson_xg + β × multiplicative_xg
05
Bivariate Poisson simulation

Scorelines are simulated using a bivariate Poisson distribution rather than two independent Poisson processes. Parameter λ₃ (default 0.1) introduces positive correlation between home and away goals, producing more realistic draw probabilities. Independent Poisson models can underestimate draws as they treat the outcomes as indipendant, wher we need to account for tactics and dynamics in football (losing teams will push forward, level/ahead teams can become more cautious) which will increase the likelyhood of draws like 1-1 and 2-2.

P(X=i, Y=j) = Σₖ [ Poisson(i−k; λ₁) × Poisson(j−k; λ₂) × Poisson(k; λ₃) ]

where λ₁ = home_xg − λ₃, λ₂ = away_xg − λ₃, and the sum is over k = 0..min(i,j). This yields a 8×8 scoreline probability matrix which is normalised to sum to 1.

06
Outcome probability

Match outcome probabilities are derived from the scoreline matrix:

P(home win) = Σᵢ₊ⱼ P(i,j) where i (home) > j (away)
P(draw) = Σᵢ₊ⱼ P(i,j) where i = j
P(away win) = Σᵢ₊ⱼ P(i,j) where j > i

Clean sheet probabilities are derived as P(away goals = 0) for home clean sheet and P(home goals = 0) for away clean sheet. Over 2.5 probability is Σ P(i,j) where i+j > 2.

{# ── Section 2: League simulation ── #}

League table simulation

01
Monte Carlo simulation

The remaining unplayedfixtures are simulated by generating a random outcome from the model's home win / draw / away win probability distribution. Points are awarded per team off the back of these simulated results and stored.

02
10,000 runs

The full remaining season is simulated 10,000 times. Each run produces a final league table. The number of times a team finishes in each position gives the team's final league position probability. Expected final points (xPTS) are calculated as current points plus the average simulated points from the entire sim.

03
Limitations

The simulation uses static probabilities, meaning it does not update the match predictions during the sim. Probabilities should be interpreted as reflecting current team strength at the time of table generation.

{# ── Section 4: Data sources ── #}

Data sources

Understat
Match-level xG, shot coordinates, player shot data. Used for all xG-based calculations and shotmap generation.
FBref & StatsBomb
Player-level statistics including goals, assists, npxG, xA, minutes played. Used for player radar charts and comparison tool.
CheckTheChance
Bookie-implied probabilities (margin-adjusted) for match outcomes and clean sheets. Used in the bet value finder to calculate model edge.
